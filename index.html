Diet

An unhealthy diet is one of the major risk factors for a range of chronic diseases, including cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes and other conditions linked to obesity. Specific recommendations for a healthy diet include: eating more fruit, vegetables, legumes, nuts and grains; cutting down on salt, sugar and fats. It is also advisable to choose unsaturated fats, instead of saturated fats and towards the elimination of trans-fatty acids.

Dietary Habits

Nutrition

Nutrition is the study of nutrients in food, how the body uses nutrients, and the relationship between diet, health, and disease. Vitamins and minerals are food components that help support overall health and play important roles in cell metabolism and neurological functions. Vitamins aid in energy production, wound healing, bone formation, immunity, and eye and skin health. Minerals help maintain cardiovascular health and provide structure to the skeleton.

Building Blocks

Nutrients are considered as building blocks of a human body. Nutrients helps in repairing damaged skin, blood, and other body parts of a human being. Calcium is also a building block for building bones and Iron is a building block for blood.

Nutrition Habits

Nutrition habits will inevitably give you a leaner body that will be ‘insulin sensitive’. There has been a lot of focus on sugar and this is the main reason why insulin sensitivity in your body has the ability to deal with sugar/glucose properly, using it in the right way, for energy, as opposed to storing it as fat.
